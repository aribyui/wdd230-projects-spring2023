Demographic Data

Nuevo León, officially the Free and Sovereign State of Nuevo León, is one of the thirty-one states that, together with Mexico City, makeup Mexico. Its capital and most populous city is Monterrey. It is divided into fifty-one municipalities. It is located in the northeast of the country, bordered to the north by the Rio Grande that separates it from the United States, to the east by Tamaulipas, to the south by San Luis Potosí, and to the west by Coahuila and Zacatecas. 📍 Location Map

Cerro de la Silla

The Cerro de la Silla is a mountain and natural monument located within the metropolitan area of the city of Monterrey, Nuevo León, in northeastern Mexico. Named for its distinctive saddle-shaped profile when viewed from the west, it is a well-known symbol of the city of Monterrey, despite being located in the adjacent municipality of Guadalupe.

It covers an area of 60.5 square kilometres (23 mi2). The mountain has four peaks: Pico Antena, Pico Norte, Pico Sur and Pico la Virgen; Pico Norte (North Peak) is the highest at 1,820 m (5,970 ft) while Pico la Virgen (Virgin's Peak) is the lowest at 1,750 m (5,740 ft).

Fundidora Park

Fundidora Park is a public park located in the city of Monterrey, Nuevo León, 3 and is located on land occupied by the Monterrey Iron and Steel Foundry Company from 1900 to 1986. Among the attractions of the fundadora park are the following: Steel Museum Horno 3, Baseball Museum, Wax Museum, Ice Skating Rink, Zip Line and Bike Zipline, Green Areas, Excercise Area, Banamex Auditorium, El Lingote Restaurant, Tenerias Restaurant.

Santa Lucía Riverwalk

Santa Lucía Riverwalk is a canal or artificial river and pedestrian route that is located in the first square of the city of Monterrey, Nuevo León, northeast of Mexico. The work (inspired by the River Walk in San Antonio, Texas) was inaugurated on September 15, 2007 by the President of the Republic, Felipe Calderón Hinojosa; the governor of the state of Nuevo León, José Natividad González Parás; and the mayor of the city of Monterrey, Adalberto Madero.

Arena Monterrey

Arena Monterrey is an indoor arena in Monterrey, Mexico. It is primarily used for shows, concerts and indoor sports like indoor soccer, arena football and basketball. It used to be the home arena of the Monterrey Fury indoor soccer team and the Fuerza Regia, a professional basketball team in the Liga Nacional de Baloncesto Profesional; the Monterrey La Raza, a team in the NISL; and the Monterrey Steel, an indoor American football team in the National Arena League.

García Caves

Grutas de García (Garcia Caves) is a cave complex located 9 km outside García, Nuevo León in northern Mexico about 30 km from Monterrey. The caves are inside Sierra del Fraile mountain and protected area. The easiest way to reach the entrance to the caves is by taking a five-minute ride on the aerial tramway, but there is a steep path that can be taken to reach the caves. 📍 Location Map
